**Tweet 2**
First, a quick refresher.

Fair Housing prohibits language that signals preference or discourages protected classes from applying.

You probably know the obvious stuff: no "great for families," no "quiet Christian neighborhood."

But AI makes a different kind of mistake. A sneakier one.

---

**Tweet 3**
I asked ChatGPT to write a listing for a property with a "cozy, walkable neighborhood perfect for young professionals."

Sounds harmless, right?

"Young professionals" is age-coded language.

That phrase could flag as age discrimination under Fair Housing. Your broker could be on the hook.

---

**Tweet 4**
Here's why this happens:

ChatGPT is trained to write what sounds good.

It has no housing law context. No protected class filters. No awareness that "master bedroom" is flagged in some markets. No understanding that "ideal for empty nesters" is discriminatory.

It just... writes.

---

**Tweet 5**
The 7 protected classes under Fair Housing:

• Race
• Color
• National origin
• Religion
• Sex
• Familial status
• Disability

State/local laws add more: age, marital status, source of income, sexual orientation.

AI tools trained on general web data don't know your state's rules.

---

**Tweet 6**
Some real examples of AI-generated phrases that can trigger violations:

❌ "Perfect for the active lifestyle"
❌ "Walking distance to top-rated schools" (familial status signal)
❌ "Ideal for a growing family"
❌ "Quiet, established neighborhood"
❌ "Handicap accessible" (use "accessible features" instead)
